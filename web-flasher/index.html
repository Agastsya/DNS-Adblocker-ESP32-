PocketDNS

Network ad & tracker blocker for ESP32

Install PocketDNS onto your ESP32 right here in your browser — no software to download, no command line. Plug the board in with a USB cable, click Install, and pick your board when asked.

This browser can't flash over USB.
Web Serial only works in Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, or Opera on a desktop or laptop — not Safari, not Firefox, and not phones/tablets. Open this same page in Chrome or Edge and the Install button will appear.
This page must be opened over https:// for USB flashing to work.

You don't need the board connected yet — click Install, then select your ESP32's port from the list that appears.

  1. Plug in your ESP32Connect the board to this computer with a USB data cable (not a charge-only one).
  2. Click “Connect” and installPick your board's serial port from the list, then let it flash. Takes about a minute. If it won't connect, hold the board's BOOT button while it starts.
  3. Join the “PocketDNS-Setup” Wi-FiAfter flashing, the board creates its own Wi-Fi network. On your phone or laptop, connect to PocketDNS-Setup.
  4. Enter your home Wi-FiA setup page opens automatically — type your home Wi-Fi name and password. The board saves it and restarts, joining your network.
  5. Point your devices at itSet your phone/laptop/router's DNS to the board's IP (shown on your router), then open http://<that-ip>/ for the dashboard. Full instructions in the README.
Heads up: PocketDNS blocks ad & tracker domains network-wide (like Pi-hole). It cannot block YouTube ads or ads served from a page's own domain — no DNS-based blocker can. Why?
